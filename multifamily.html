Common Questions

FAQs About Selling Apartment Buildings & Multifamily

Straightforward answers for multifamily owners considering a direct sale.

How do you value an apartment building?

We value multifamily properties primarily on net operating income (NOI) and the prevailing market cap rate. Beyond the headline numbers, we evaluate unit mix, current rents versus achievable market rents, occupancy trends, physical condition, capital expenditure needs, tenant quality, and location fundamentals like job growth, population trends, and rental demand. For value-add properties, we underwrite to stabilized income — factoring in achievable rent increases after renovations, vacancy reduction through improved management, and operational cost savings. This approach often results in a stronger offer than buyers who price strictly on trailing 12-month numbers.

Do you buy apartment buildings with tenants in place?

Yes. We buy multifamily properties at any occupancy level — fully leased, partially occupied, or completely vacant. We assume all existing leases at closing and take over the tenant relationship from day one. Non-paying tenants, problem tenants, and pending evictions all become our responsibility after closing. You don't need to resolve any tenant issues, complete any evictions, or wait for leases to expire before selling to us.

What's the smallest multifamily property you'll buy?

We buy duplexes, triplexes, fourplexes, and everything larger. There is no minimum unit count. That said, the majority of our multifamily acquisitions are 5+ unit properties because that's where commercial valuation and financing apply. If you have a small 2–4 unit property you want to sell quickly for cash, we're happy to evaluate it with the same speed and seriousness as a 100-unit complex.

How fast can you close on a multifamily property?

We typically close on multifamily acquisitions in 14 to 30 days from executed contract, depending on deal size, title complexity, and the scope of due diligence. For smaller properties with clean title and straightforward financials, we can move even faster. We provide proof of funds upfront, don't condition on financing, and don't re-trade after going under contract. When we commit, we close.

Do you buy apartment buildings in any state?

Yes. We acquire multifamily properties in all 50 states. Each market has its own rent dynamics, landlord-tenant laws, and regulatory environment — including rent control, just-cause eviction, and housing code requirements — and our underwriting accounts for all of it. We have relationships with multifamily-experienced property managers, contractors, and lenders across the country. Location doesn't limit us — deal quality does.

Do I need to make repairs before selling my apartment building?

No. We buy multifamily properties in any condition — deferred maintenance, aging mechanical systems, cosmetic neglect, code violations, fire damage, or units that need full gut renovations. We factor all repair and renovation costs into our offer and handle every improvement after closing. You don't need to invest a single dollar into the property before selling. In fact, we prefer to manage renovations ourselves so we can control the scope, timeline, and quality.
