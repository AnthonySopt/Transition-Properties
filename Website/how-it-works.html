Step 2

Receive a Fair Cash Offer Within 24 Hours

Within 24 hours of your first contact, we present a written cash offer. Here's how we arrive at the number:

We research recent comparable sales in your area, evaluate the property's current condition and estimate repair costs, factor in holding and closing costs we'll incur, and arrive at a price that's fair for both sides.

We show you every number. You'll see the comps we used, the repairs we're estimating, and how we calculated our offer. No black-box pricing, no "take it or leave it" tactics. If our number doesn't work for you, there's absolutely no pressure to accept — and we'll tell you honestly if listing with an agent might get you a better result.

Our offers don't expire. Take the time you need to think it over, talk to family, or compare options. We'll be here when you're ready.

Step 3

Close on Your Timeline & Get Paid

Once you accept the offer, here's what happens:

We open with a title company. We use a reputable, licensed title company to handle the closing. They conduct the title search, prepare the deed, coordinate payoffs for any existing mortgages or liens, and ensure everything is legally clean.

You choose the closing date. Need to close in 7 days? We can do that. Need 60 or 90 days to find a new place, wrap up an estate, or coordinate a move? That's perfectly fine too. You pick the date and we meet you there.

We cover standard closing costs. Title search fees, deed preparation, recording fees — we pay these. There are no agent commissions (we're not realtors) and no hidden fees. The number you agreed to is the number you receive.

You get paid. On closing day, you sign the paperwork and funds are wired directly to your bank account (or you receive a cashier's check). The title company handles disbursement of payoffs for any existing mortgage, liens, or back taxes from the sale proceeds. You walk away clean.

The entire process — from first phone call to cash in your account — can happen in as little as one week. Most of our sales close within 7 to 21 days.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Honest, detailed answers to the questions we hear most from property owners considering a cash sale.

Do cash home buyers charge fees or commissions?

No. Transition Properties charges absolutely zero fees and zero commissions. There are no agent commissions (we're not realtors), no closing cost charges on your end, no administrative fees, and no hidden charges of any kind. We also cover standard closing costs including title search, deed preparation, and recording fees. The cash offer you accept is the amount you receive at closing. This is one of the biggest advantages of selling to a direct cash buyer instead of listing on the open market, where you'd typically pay 5–6% in agent commissions plus 2–4% in seller closing costs.

Do I need to make repairs before selling?

No. We buy properties in any condition — damaged roofs, foundation problems, mold, fire damage, outdated interiors, code violations, hoarding situations, broken HVAC systems, and everything in between. You don't need to clean, stage, repair, or fix a single thing. We handle all of that after closing. In fact, we prefer that you don't make repairs — you might spend money fixing things that don't affect our valuation. See our pages on selling your house fast and pre-foreclosure help for more detail.

How fast can I close on my house?

We can close in as little as 7 days from the time you accept our offer. The typical timeline is 7 to 21 days for residential properties. However, you choose your closing date — whether that's one week or three months from now. Some sellers need time to find a new home, coordinate a move, or wrap up an estate. You pick the date and we meet you there. For commercial properties, closings typically take 14 to 30 days due to more extensive due diligence.

What if my property has liens or title issues?

We buy properties with liens, back taxes, judgments, HOA violations, and title defects regularly. These situations don't stop us — they're a normal part of what we do. Our team works with the title company to identify and resolve all title issues as part of the closing process. Outstanding liens, delinquent taxes, and judgments are paid from the sale proceeds at closing, so you don't need any money out of pocket. If the title issues are severe enough that they can't be resolved at closing, we'll let you know upfront and discuss options.

Can I sell my property with tenants in place?

Yes. We buy tenant-occupied properties regularly — it's one of the most common situations we handle, especially from tired landlords. We assume all existing leases at closing and take over the tenant relationship from day one. Non-paying tenants, problem tenants, and pending evictions all become our responsibility. You don't need to evict anyone, wait for leases to expire, or even tell your tenants about the sale until the deal is done.

What paperwork do I need to sell my house?

Very little on your end. You'll need a valid government-issued ID and the ability to sign closing documents. The title company handles the heavy lifting: title search, deed preparation, mortgage payoff coordination, lien resolution, and closing document preparation. If the property is in probate, you'll need Letters of Administration from the court. If there's an existing mortgage, we'll need the loan account number (found on any mortgage statement). We guide you through every step — most sellers are surprised at how little they actually need to do.

How do I know if a property buyer is legitimate?

Great question — and you should absolutely do your homework before selling to anyone. Here's what to look for: Ask for proof of funds — a legitimate buyer can show you a bank statement or letter proving they have the capital. Check their online presence — website, reviews, social media. Verify their business with the state's corporate registry (Division of Corporations in Florida). Ask for references from past sellers. Make sure they use a third-party title company for closing — never wire money directly to a buyer. Transition Properties provides proof of funds upfront, closes through licensed title companies, and is happy to connect you with past sellers as references.

How does a cash offer compare to listing with a realtor?

A cash offer from a direct buyer is typically below the full retail listing price — and we're upfront about that. You're trading maximum price for speed, certainty, and zero out-of-pocket costs. However, the gap is often much smaller than people assume. Consider what a traditional listing actually costs: 5–6% agent commissions, 2–4% in seller closing costs, $5,000–$30,000 in repairs and staging to make the home market-ready, 3–6 months of mortgage payments while the home sits on the market, and the very real risk that a buyer's financing falls through and you start over. When you add all of that up, many sellers find that a cash offer nets them a similar or even better amount — without the stress, uncertainty, and months of waiting. We encourage you to compare both options before deciding.
